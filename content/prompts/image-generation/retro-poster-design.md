title: "Retro Vintage Poster"
description: "Create a vintage-style poster with retro typography, limited color palette, halftone textures, and aged paper aesthetics. Great for events, music, food brands, and nostalgia marketing."
tool: "nano-banana"
tags: ["retro", "vintage", "poster", "typography", "halftone", "nostalgia"]
date: "2026-04-07"
promptText: |
  Design a vintage retro poster for [POSTER SUBJECT].

  Era: [TIME PERIOD AESTHETIC]. Key visual references for the style:
  [ERA REFERENCE] poster design language — limited color palette, bold typography,
  decorative borders, and hand-crafted illustration feel.

  Layout structure:
  - Top: Bold headline "[MAIN HEADLINE]" in period-appropriate lettering
  - Center: Primary illustration or graphic element
  - Bottom: Supporting text "[SUPPORTING TEXT]"
  - Decorative border frame around entire composition

  Typography:
  Bold slab serif or condensed display typeface for headlines.
  Smaller complementary serif for body text.
  All text hand-lettered feel — slight imperfection in letterforms.
  Text fills: flat solid color or halftone dot pattern.

  Color palette: Maximum 4 colors.
  Primary: [COLOR 1]. Secondary: [COLOR 2]. Accent: [COLOR 3]. Background: [BACKGROUND COLOR].
  Colors are slightly desaturated — aged, not fresh. Slight color misregistration effect
  (0.5–1px offset) for authentic letterpress look.

  Texture: Aged paper grain overlay across entire poster (opacity 30–40%).
  Halftone dot pattern on shadow areas. Slight yellowing/browning at edges.
  Subtle foxing spots. Overall: looks like a genuine vintage find, not a modern fake.

  Illustration: Central image of [ILLUSTRATION SUBJECT] in flat 2-color lithograph style.
  No gradients — only flat fills and hatched shadows.

  Aspect ratio: 2:3 poster format. Clean white margin around all sides.
  Output feel: Museum-quality reproduction of an authentic [TIME PERIOD] poster.
usage: |
  1. Open Nano Banana
  2. Define your poster subject, time period, and content
  3. Choose 3–4 colors that feel authentic to the era
  4. Keep headline text short and punchy (3–6 words maximum)
  5. Generate and refine — vintage styles often need 2–3 iterations to nail

  Perfect for: event posters, music gig flyers, restaurant menus, product labels,
  social media throwback content, and brand identity systems
variables: |
  [POSTER SUBJECT] → What the poster promotes
    Examples: "a jazz music festival", "an artisan coffee brand", "a travel destination: New Orleans"

  [TIME PERIOD AESTHETIC] → The era you're referencing
    Options: "1920s Art Deco", "1930s WPA travel poster", "1950s American diner",
              "1960s psychedelic rock", "1970s progressive rock album",
              "1980s neon aerobics", "1990s rave flyer"

  [ERA REFERENCE] → Specific style keyword
    Examples: "Bauhaus", "Art Nouveau", "WPA Federal Art Project", "Saul Bass",
              "Push Pin Studios", "Swiss International Style"

  [MAIN HEADLINE] → Bold headline text (3–6 words)
  [SUPPORTING TEXT] → Smaller text below (date, location, tagline)
  [ILLUSTRATION SUBJECT] → Central image
  [COLOR 1–3] → Your palette. [BACKGROUND COLOR] → Base paper color (cream, tan, etc.)
tips: |
  • For more authentic aging: add "torn edges on left side, foxing spots in corners"
  • Add "offset color registration error, 1px cyan shift" for ultra-authentic letterpress look
  • "Screen printed" in the prompt pushes toward more graphic, flat-color results
  • For Art Deco: add "sunburst rays, geometric zigzag patterns, metallic gold accents"
  • For 1960s psych: add "melting letterforms, swirling background patterns, blacklight-reactive colors"
  • Add "French Art Nouveau, Alphonse Mucha style" for ornate floral border illustrations
