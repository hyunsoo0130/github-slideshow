title: "Glassmorphism 3D Typography"
description: "Create frosted glass 3D letters with light refraction, caustic patterns, and a subtle color-tinted studio background. Ideal for tech brands, app UI mockups, and modern design headers."
tool: "nano-banana"
tags: ["3d", "glass", "typography", "tech", "minimal"]
date: "2026-04-02"
promptText: |
  Create the word "[YOUR TEXT]" as a 3D glass typography sculpture.

  Form: Each letter is a thick slab of frosted borosilicate glass — semi-transparent,
  with visible light refraction inside the material. Letters stand upright,
  slightly angled toward viewer (5–10° tilt). Generous letter spacing.

  Material: Frosted glass with [COLOR] tint. The glass concentrates [COLOR]
  in dense central areas; edges become nearly clear. Surface has subtle ground-glass
  micro-texture — not mirror smooth. Sharp specular edge highlights catch studio light.

  Light behavior: Internal caustic patterns visible on background surface.
  Light bends visibly through the glass, creating prismatic dispersion at thin edges.
  Reflection: glass reflects a clean, blurred environment — no harsh reflections.
  Transmission: ~60–70% transparent in thin areas, ~90% opaque in dense letter cores.

  Background: Soft gradient from pale [COLOR] (top) to white (bottom).
  Subtle caustic shadow patterns cast on the background from the glass letters.
  No hard drop shadows — only soft diffused light pools.

  Lighting: Polarized soft box from upper-left, 5600K daylight.
  Subtle fill from right. No harsh specular blooms.

  Camera: Slight 3/4 angle, 15° elevation. Product photography composition.
  Aspect ratio: 3:2 landscape. Sharp focus throughout.

  Output feel: Luxury brand identity shoot. Premium tech aesthetic.
usage: |
  1. Open Nano Banana in your browser
  2. Replace [YOUR TEXT] with your word (1–3 words)
  3. Replace [COLOR] with a light, translucent-friendly color:
     - "sky blue", "mint green", "lavender", "rose", "amber" work especially well
  4. For best results, use light/pastel color names rather than dark ones
  5. Generate and select the most optically interesting variation

  This prompt works exceptionally well for: tech product names, app names, SaaS brands
variables: |
  [YOUR TEXT] → Word or phrase to render as glass letters. Works best: 1–2 words, 4–8 letters each
  [COLOR] → A light, translucent color. Best results with:
    - "sky blue" / "azure"
    - "pale lavender"
    - "mint green"
    - "warm rose"
    - "golden amber"
    - "icy cyan"
  Avoid very dark colors — they reduce the glass transparency effect
tips: |
  • Pastel and light colors produce the most convincing glass effect
  • Add "frosted privacy glass" to the material for a more diffused, premium look
  • For a darker version: specify "smoked glass" instead of "frosted borosilicate"
  • Request "iridescent film on surface" for a rainbow-prismatic oil-slick glass effect
  • Combine with product photography by adding "[product name] visible through the glass" at the end
  • For sharper caustics, add "hard afternoon sunlight through glass, sharp caustic patterns"
